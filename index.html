Filipino Spaghetti Recipe

History

The heavily loved Filipino dish dates back as early as the 1940s-1960s. During the American Commonwealth Period, there was a shortage of tomatos in World War II which lead to the development of banana ketchup. The Americans then introduced the traditional Italian cuisine, Spaghetti, to the people in the Philippine Islands. With the addition of Banana Ketchup, soon after, it was tweaked according to the Filipino's taste for sweet dishes.

This recipe makes an easy-simple Filipino Spaghetti; the uniqueness of tasting the smoky hotdogs alongside the sweet-cheesy sauce is what make it so loved by the Filipinos!

Ingredients

  • Uncooked Spaghetti (1 Pound)
  • Oil (1 Tablespoon)
  • Filipino-Style Hotdogs (4, Diagonally Sliced)
  • Onion (1, Peeled and Chopped)
  • Garlic Cloves (3, Peeled and Minced)
  • Bell Pepper (1, Seeded, Cored, and Chopped)
  • Ground Beef (1 Pound)
  • Tomato Sauce (2 Cups)
  • Tomato Paste (1/2 Cup)
  • Banana Ketchup (1 Cup, Preferable Sweet and Spicy)
  • Beef Broth (1 Cup)
  • Sugar (1 Teaspoon)
  • Salt & Pepper (To Taste)
  • Quick-Melt Cheese (1/2 Cup, Shredded)

Directions

  1. In a pot over medium heat, cook spaghetti in salted boiling water according to package's direction for about 7 to 9 minutes or until firm. Drain right after.
  2. In a large saucepan, heat oil. Add sliced hot dogs and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 1 to 2 minutes or until lightly browned. Remove from pan and set aside.
  3. Add onions, garlic, and bell peppers to pan. Cook until softened.
  4. Add ground beek and cook, stirring occasionally and breaking into small pieces until lightly browned. Drain all excess fat.
  5. Add tomato sauce, tomato paste, banana ketchup, and beef broth.
  6. While stirring, gradually add in sugar.
  7. Bring to a boil and the lower heat to simmer, covered, for about one hour or until meat is fully cooked an sauce is thickened. If the sauce is getting too thick, add water in 1/2 cup increments as needed.
  8. During the last 15 minutes of cooking, add browned hot dogs. Season with salt and pepper to taste (preferablly less since the cheese is already salty).
  9. To serve, spoon spaghetti sauce over noodles and top with how ever much cheese you desire!
